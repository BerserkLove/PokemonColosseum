⚔️ Pokémon Colosseum

Welcome to the Pokémon Colosseum!

Can you conquer the Colosseum and become the best there ever was? Build your team, follow your strategy, discover hidden power dynamics, and push your draft beyond normal limits.

Goal

Draft a team with the highest possible final League Power. Your Pokémon’s BST is the base, but smart choices, items, quests, concepts, rare finds and PokéMart preparation can push the score much higher.

How a run works

Choose a trainer, item and difficulty. Then draft six Pokémon from blind options. Be aware of Grunts causing chaos. After the draft, your team faces Gym Leaders, the Elite Four, the Champion and the very best.

Items

Items bend the rules. Starting items shape the run, while PokéMart X-Items are one-run Backpack tools you can buy with coins to reroll, guard, or boost your team during pick phases.

Quests

Hidden quests are secret interactions. Some fuse Pokémon, awaken forms, unlock special bonuses, or change the draft itself. Some quests are hidden, and you are not expected to know all of them at the start.

Team Concepts

A strong theme can earn concept points. Examples include type-focused teams, Regi cores, starters, Paradox Pokémon, Ultra Beasts, regional teams and other strange little team identities.

Rare Pokémon

Shiny Pokémon can appear and grant bonus points. Special Pokémon like Light Ball Pikachu and Eternal Floette are very rare and can turn a normal run into a trophy run.

PokéMart & Coins

After a completed run, you earn coins equal to 10% of your final League Power. Easy, Normal and Master each have their own Coin Pouch, so coins earned in one difficulty stay in that difficulty.

During the draft you can visit the PokéMart once per pick phase. Buy one-run X-Items for your Backpack to reroll, guard Pokémon from Grunts, or apply targeted score bonuses. Most X-Items target one eligible Pokémon; X All affects the full team once complete. The Backpack holds 2 items and starts empty on every new run.

Difficulty

Normal Mode is the standard climb. Master Mode gives you the Ultimate Challenge. Master Mode gets enabled after you have beaten the Pokémon Colosseum once on Normal Mode.

History & Codex

History saves past runs and can be cleared. The Collection Log is separate: it tracks discovered quests, concepts, shinies, special Pokémon and achievements across runs.

You can open this again anytime with the Intro button. The toggle is saved in this browser.
